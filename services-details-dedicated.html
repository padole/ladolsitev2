Dedicated Equipment & Port Facilities

Overview

Engineered for Efficiency. Equipped for Every Operation.

At LADOL, we provide world-class equipment and port infrastructure designed to support the most demanding offshore, marine, and industrial operations. From heavy-lift cranes and dedicated warehousing to quayside logistics and storage systems, our facilities form the operational backbone for seamless project execution.

Engineered for safety, reliability, and efficiency, our assets enable clients to manage high-volume, heavy-duty, and precision-based tasks with confidence. With a skilled workforce and 24/7 operational readiness, LADOL delivers the tools and infrastructure needed to keep offshore and onshore projects running smoothly and safely.

Seamless Integration with Port & Marine Services

Our equipment and port facilities work hand-in-hand with LADOL’s broader logistics and marine service ecosystem.

This integration ensures faster cargo handling, reduced transportation costs, and safer project execution—enabling clients to focus on core operations while we handle the complexity.

Safety, Reliability & Compliance

Every LADOL asset is maintained to international standards, including ISO, ISPS, and HSE requirements.

Routine inspections, preventive maintenance, and continuous staff training guarantee safe, reliable, and efficient operations around the clock.

Facility Features

Dedicated Storage & Warehousing

Enclosed areas, pipe racks, and stacking zones for organised and secure material management.

Integrated Fuel & Power Systems

Onsite fuel storage, compressors, and generators ensuring uninterrupted operations.

Comprehensive Transport Fleet

Forklifts, trucks, trailers, and transporters for efficient cargo movement.

Certified Personnel & Safety Systems

Experienced operators, riggers, and logistics specialists adhering to ISO, ISPS, and HSE standards.

ABOUT

Positioned strategically at the entry point to Lagos harbor, LADOL provides a one-stop-shop for multinational industrial and oil and gas companies operating in West Africa.
